Judging License Levels

A hierarchy system based on knowledge and experience

0

Level 0 $29

The entry point into USA Streetlifting judging. This course covers the full standards for the Pull and the Dip — what counts as a good rep, what a no-rep looks like, and how to deliver a clear call.

Entry · Pull & Dip
1

Level 1 $39

Builds on Level 0 and adds the Muscle Up and the Back Squat. After completing this course you are qualified to judge all four competition movements at any sanctioned USA Streetlifting meet.

Foundational · All 4 Movements
2

Level 2 Coming Soon

Extends Level 1 with complete knowledge of technical infractions, equipment rules, and Head Judge responsibilities. This certification will be required to serve as Head Judge at sanctioned competitions.

Advanced · Head Judge
3

Level 3

Not a course certification. Awarded exclusively based on proven experience — consistently accurate judging and strict rule enforcement across multiple sanctioned meets.

Experience Only

About

USA Streetlifting
Judge Certification

Building a nationwide network of certified judges who uphold a single, unified standard — at every meet, in every state.

🎯

Our Mission

Judges are foundational to the integrity and growth of the sport. As streetlifting evolves into a nationally recognized discipline, athletes must be evaluated under a unified, objective standard — regardless of location or event size.

🌎

Our Vision

Develop a nationwide network of certified judges who are trained, tested, and consistently held to high standards. These individuals ensure fairness, uphold technical standards, and elevate the legitimacy of the sport across every sanctioned meet — year after year.

How Judging Works

3

Judges Per Attempt

Every attempt is evaluated by three certified judges simultaneously, each issuing an independent decision.

3/3

Unanimous Required

For most criteria, all 3 judges must agree for the lift to count. A single no-rep call on any standard criteria fails the attempt. A 2/3 majority applies only to shoulder depth (dip) and squat depth.

3

Attempts Per Lift

Each athlete gets three attempts per lift to achieve their best result.

General Standards

◆

Strict Form

No kipping, swinging, or momentum-assisted movement on any lift.

◆

Judge Signals

Athletes must wait for and respond to all Head Judge commands — missing a signal results in a no-rep.

◆

Equipment

Approved weight belt required. Chalk and wrist wraps allowed. Gloves are not permitted.

◆

Weight Classes

Athletes compete in bodyweight-based divisions. Weigh-ins take place the morning of competition.

This is a summary only. All technical standards, edge cases, and complete judging criteria are defined in the official rulebook.
